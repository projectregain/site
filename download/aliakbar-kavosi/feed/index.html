A camarilla point is a price level of significance in technical analysis of financial markets that is used by traders as a predictive indicator of market movement. A camarilla point is calculated as an average of significant prices (High, Low and Close) from the performance of a market in the prior trading period. If the market in the following period trades above the camarilla point, it is usually evaluated as a bullish sentiment, whereas trading below the camarilla point is seen as bearish.
