MIDAS which is an acronym for Market Interpretation/Data Analysis System is the innovative work of the late technical analyst Paul Levine. Levine strongly believed that the dynamic interplay of support and resistance (S/R) and accumulation and distribution are the ultimate determinants of price behavior. After examining and rejecting all of the standard technical analysis techniques for determining support and resistance, he came up with a new approach – MIDAS.

The instructions and information below are at its very best a basic understanding of the MIDAS Technical Analysis System. It is highly recommended that traders interested in the MIDAS Indicators download the “MIDAS Technical Analysis: A VWAP Approach to Trading and Investing in Todays Markets” – by Andrew Coles and David Hawkins to learn more about the MIDAS Technical Analysis System.

### MIDAS Support/Resistance Curves

The MIDAS+High+Low curves form a channel that represents dynamic S/R in the market. It works exceptionally well for capturing pullbacks from impulses and/or breakouts.

When and How to Use the MIDAS+High+Low Curves

  1. Identify an accelerated price move (impulse and/or breakout) in any market manually or with the International Capital Flow Model.

    1. Launch a MIDAS S/R curve from the start of the accelerated move.
    2. Wait for price to pull all the way back to the MIDAS S/R curve.
    3. Look for any type of odds enhancer that price will react to the MIDAS S/R curve, such as a divergence (in overbought/oversold conditions), etc.
    4. Enter a position with a Stop Loss above/below the MIDAS S/R curve.
